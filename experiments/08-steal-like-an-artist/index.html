THE CREATIVE MANIFESTO

Every artist gets asked the question, "Where do you get your ideas?"

The honest artist answers, "I steal them."

Nothing is original. All creative work builds on what came before. An artist is a collector, not a hoarder. You are the sum of your influences.

01

Steal Like An Artist

Good Theft vs. Bad Theft

GOOD THEFT

  • Honor
  • Study
  • Steal from many
  • Credit
  • Transform

BAD THEFT

  • Degrade
  • Skim
  • Steal from one
  • Plagiarize
  • Imitate

"Steal from one, it's plagiarism; steal from many, it's research."

02

DON'T WAIT UNTIL YOU KNOW WHO YOU ARE TO GET STARTED

FAKE IT 'TIL YOU MAKE IT

You have to dress for the job you want, not the job you have. Start doing the work you want to be doing before you feel ready.

COPY YOUR HEROES

Imitation is not flattery. It's practice. By failing to copy your heroes, you find where you differ. That's where your own style lives.

03

WRITE THE BOOK YOU WANT TO READ

"Draw the art you want to see, start the business you want to run, play the music you want to hear."

Solve your own problems. Fill your own gaps.

04

USE YOUR HANDS

The Analog Desk

Where you play, doodle, and scratch things out. No screens allowed. This is where the magic starts.

The Digital Desk

Where you edit, publish, and polish. Once the idea is born analog, bring it here to finish.

05

SIDE PROJECTS & HOBBIES

Practice Productive Procrastination.

The work you do while you're procrastinating is probably the work you should be doing for the rest of your life.

Don't throw any of yourself away. If you have two or three passions, don't feel like you have to pick one. Let them talk to each other.

06

DO GOOD WORK AND SHARE IT WITH PEOPLE

THE SECRET FORMULA:

STEP 1: DO GOOD WORK.
(this takes a long time)

STEP 2: SHARE IT.
(post it on the internet)
07

GEOGRAPHY IS NO LONGER OUR MASTER

You don't need to live in New York or London to be part of the creative scene. You can build your own world from your bedroom.

YOUR STUDIO IS A PORTAL
08

BE NICE. (THE WORLD IS A SMALL TOWN)

  • Make friends, ignore enemies.
  • Write fan letters to your heroes.
  • Don't look for validation, look for inspiration.
09

BE BORING. (IT'S THE ONLY WAY)

THE CALENDAR

"Keep a visual record of your streaks. Don't break the chain."

THE LOGBOOK

"List what you did today. It's not a diary, it's a record of tiny wins."

Creativity requires routine. Establish a practice so you can get the work done without thinking about it.

10

CREATIVITY IS SUBTRACTION

"In this age of information abundance, those who get ahead will be the folks who figure out what to leave out."

LIMIT YOURSELF.

Constraints are freedom. Use only one color. Write in 100 words. Build a world with 3 rules.
